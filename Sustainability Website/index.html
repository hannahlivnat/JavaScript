The Zero Waste Journey

  • Learn About Sustainability

    The first step to change is expanding our knowledge on our own individual impact.

  • What's Causing Your Waste Impact?

    Evaluate yourself. Are you a fashionista? A take-out food lover? A collector of 5 million hair products? Determining what causes the majority of your waste gives you an impactful starting point.

  • Use Up and Replace

    Don't throw everything unsustainable away, you'll just be adding to the landfill. It's okay to go slow! Use up your hair products and then replace it with a sustainable alternative. It will be better for the planet and your budget!

  • Expand Slowly in Your Household

    After you've addressed the highest priority items, start to look at the other aspects of your life that cause waste. What's left in your trashcan now, and how can you reduce it?

  • Where are you Shopping?

    Who needs a trash can anymore? Now that you're a pro in your own household and your personal waste is greatly reduced, start assessing where you spend your money. Are you buying dinner at restaurants that throw away pounds of plastic and unused food everyday? Are you frequenting stores that should have their own landfill? You've already changed your personal habits, now start impacting the community with your wallet!

  • Pass It On

    No one likes a preacher, but there are ways to impact your friends and family by simply modeling sustainable living. Engage in gentle conversations with your friends, buy sustainable presents that your family will still love. Becoming a model through your actions will slowly have an impact on those around you, and you can always be ready to engage in a conversation about sustainability!
